How often do crimes go unreported?

We analyzed data from the National Crime Victimization Survey (NCVS). The NCVS, administered by the Bureau of Justice Statistics, is a national survey of approximately 49,000 to 77,400 households twice a year in the United States, on teh frequency of crime victimization, as well as characteristics and consequences of victimization. The survey focuses on gathering information on teh following crimes: assault, burglary, larceny, motor vehicle theft, rape, and robbery.

Unreported crime as a whole has roughly remained the same since 1993. There was a drop in the 2000s, but the trend has started upwards again. Why is this happening?

Let's take a closer look at what makes up the rest of the unreported crime data.
